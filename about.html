"I shut my eyes and all the world drops dead;
I lift my eyes and all is born again." — Sylvia Plath, The Bell Jar

Why I do what I do

I've never really been able to shake those words.

Not because they're sad, but because they're a constant reminder of how differently we all experience the exact same world. You can put two people in the same classroom, the same house or the same conversation, and they will walk away with completely different core memories. It all just depends on what they were already carrying.

I see it happen every single day.

Children often inherit stories about themselves long before they're old enough to question them.

"Too loud." "Too difficult." "Too quiet." "Too sensitive."

Those labels settle in deep, and after a while, they stop sounding like someone else's opinion and just start sounding like the absolute truth.

I find myself wondering how different a life could look if someone had just stepped in and interrupted that narrative a little sooner. And it doesn't take some grand, cinematic gesture or perfect, life-altering advice. It's usually just about looking at a young person and seeing who they actually are, rather than who the world decided they were.

It's so easy to dismiss the quiet moments — a quick ten-minute chat, a smile that genuinely says "I'm just really glad you're here," or remembering some random detail a child mentioned weeks ago. None of it feels like a big deal when it's happening... but those are the exact moments that tend to echo for years.

We end up becoming part of each other's stories way more often than we realise.

I never know which conversation is going to stick, or which tiny act of kindness might be the turning point for someone. I just know that every young person deserves to encounter people who look past their behaviour and actually see the person underneath.

I guess that's why that quote has stayed with me for so long.

Sometimes, the world doesn't change because everything around us suddenly shifts. Sometimes, it changes simply because, for the very first time, someone finally feels seen.

How the resources are made

✍️

From practice

Everything here comes from a youth advocate's professional standpoint: real one-to-one emotional and wellbeing support with young people facing anxiety, low mood, school avoidance and more. Every resource is shaped by what actually works in those sessions — supportive tools made with care, not medical or therapeutic advice.
